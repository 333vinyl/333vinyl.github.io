333vinyl Best Of 2014

31 December 2014 on best-of-2014

Ben Bailey's Top Albums

  1. Spoon - They Want My Soul
    An outstanding album from an outstandingly consistent band.
  2. St. Vincent - St. Vincent
    St. Vincent's best and bravest work yet.
  3. Flying Lotus - You're Dead!
    A future jazz masterpiece.
  4. Cloud Nothings - Here And Nowhere Else
    Even better and more concise than Attack On Memory.
  5. Iceage - Plowing Into The Field Of Love
    The post-punk band takes a dark western turn.
  6. The New Pornographers - Brill Bruisers
    The best New Pornographers album since Twin Cinema.
  7. TV On The Radio - Seeds
    A great comeback album from the band after their bassist died after the recording of Nine Types of Light.
  8. Parquet Courts - Content Nausea
    The second and better of two albums this band released this year. The title track is a standout, but the whole album is full of complex meditations on modern anxieties.
  9. Ought - More Than Any Other Day
    One of the most innovative new bands this year. The vocals and lyrics are Byrne-esque.
  10. The Antlers - Familiars
    Not quite as good as their previous two albums, Hospice and Burst Apart, but still a great album with an excellent vocal performance. Incorporates horns nicely.
